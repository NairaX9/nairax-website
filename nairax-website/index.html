The Problem

Africans pay too much
to move their own money

The global financial system was not built with Africa in mind. Sending money home, paying for global services, or getting paid internationally costs Nigerians billions in fees every year.

💸

High Remittance Fees

Traditional services charge 5–15% to send money to Nigeria. That money should stay with families, not middlemen.

🚫

Blocked from Global Payments

Nigerian cards are rejected on most international platforms. Freelancers and businesses lose real income daily.

📉

Currency Instability

Naira devaluation erodes savings. People need a stable, useful digital alternative for everyday transactions.

⏳

Slow Settlement Times

Bank transfers between Nigeria and diaspora can take days. In a mobile-first world, that is unacceptable.
